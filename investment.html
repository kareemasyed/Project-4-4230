Investment Banking

Description

Investment banking is a financial service that helps clients, such as corporations, governments, and institutions, raise capital and manage complex financial transactions.



  • Underwriting: Helping clients raise money through the issuance of debt or equity securities, or through Initial Public Offerings (IPOs)
  • Examining financial statements: Ensuring the accuracy of a company's financial statements and publishing a prospectus for investors
  • Facilitating transactions: Helping clients with mergers and acquisitions (M&A), reorganizations, and broker trades
  • Investment banks are often broker-dealers, which means they also help institutional investors buy and sell securities. They are regulated by the Securities and Exchange Commission, and some are also regulated as commercial banks.
