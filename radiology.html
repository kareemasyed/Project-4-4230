Radiology

Description

Radiology is a medical specialty that uses imaging technology to diagnose and treat diseases in humans and other animals. It's also known as diagnostic imaging. Radiologists are physicians who specialize in interpreting the results of imaging exams.



  • Radiology can be divided into two areas: diagnostic radiology and interventional radiology.
  • X-ray, MRI, Ultrasound, CT scan, PET scan, Bone mineral densitometry, Fluoroscopy, Mammography, and Nuclear medicine.
  • Radiology is essential for diagnosing many diseases, particularly cancer. Early diagnosis can save lives
  • It usually takes at least 13 years to become a radiologist, including undergraduate education, medical school, a four-year residency, and a one- or two-year fellowship.
